How time & people affected The Old Testament


As clearly stated by Ovid -

"All things can corrupt when minds are prone to evil."


Definition of Corruption:

In this context, corruption is rarely understood as literal, physical alteration of the text, but rather as misinterpretation of intent.

  • The "Telephone" Transmission (Scribal Errors)

Before the printing press (invented in the 15th century), the Old Testament was copied by hand. Imagine a bored scribe in 100 AD copying a manuscript late at night. He might accidentally skip a line, repeat a verse, or make a spelling mistake.




These small changes, if not corrected by a master editor, could get passed down to the next copy, making the "copy of a copy" different from the original.

  • The "Editor's Pen" (Deliberate Alteration):

    • Theological Tweaking

Early copyists sometimes added, removed, or changed words to clarify a passage or to make it align with their interpretation of the divine.

    • Cultural Contextualization

As Israel's language (Hebrew) changed over centuries, later scribes might update old archaic words, changing the original wording to be understood by their generation.

The Old Testament was born out of a turbulent history.
